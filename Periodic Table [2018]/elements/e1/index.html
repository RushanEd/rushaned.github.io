Brief information

Hydrogen is the first element in the periodic table and the lightest element as we know. It is highly inflammable(explosive) and therefore people use hydrogen as a fuel. But you won't see a vehicle runs with hydrogen due to it's high reactivity (not in near future). In some occations, hydrogen is used as a fuel. Mostly when we don't think much about safety.

Where it's used

As a rocket fuel

Hydrogen is widely used in rocket engineering as a fuel. From the beginning of the rocket to now, still it's fuel is hydrogen (solidified). Hydrogen is the perfect fuel for rocket because of many reasons.

  • Hydrogen releases a massive energy.
  • It's the lightest element, so it's easy to carry it from here to the space.
  • Hydrogen can be found easily.
  • It's eco-friendly. The byproduct of hydrogen flame is, pure water.

In rockets, hydrogens are mixed with oxygen, so that it can be fired even in space. Robert Goddard and Constantine Tsiolkovski are both inventors of that method. We still use that.

To fill air baloons

Back in the days, people used hydrogen to fill air baloons. Baloons had a low density because, hydrogen is less heavier than the atmosphere. So, the baloons lifted up. But today, we don't fill hydrogen to air baloons because, it's not safety. As hydrogen is extremely inflammable, it couldn't be used to lift air bloons. So, people replaced hydrogen with helium which is also light but non reactive.

Hydrogen bombs

Hydrogen bombs are more powerful than atom bombs. A hydrogen bomb can destroy every hydrogen atom that is near to it. That's exactly what happens inside the Sun. These bombs are radioactive when they explode.

Hydrogen metal

Hydrogen metal was made recently by scientists. They are very useful in computer science. A hydrogen metal can be used as a super conducter. It means it doesn't waste energy when electricity flows through it. But, it's still hard to make.
